Zhuldyz Datka Zhuldyz is a PhD student in Archaeological Anthropology, with a research focus on understanding the daily lives, social structures, and interactions of postoralisms in Central Asia. Her work involves the analysis of ancient pottery and soil samples to investigate past cultures and settlements. Additionally, she examines organic remains, such as food residues, to gain insights into ancient diet. Her hobbies are playing piano, tennis, golf, and swimming. She has a strong passion for travel, particularly in exploring new regions, learning foreign languages, and gaining a deeper understanding of diverse cultures, histories, and their hospitality.
